"Idle" is the ultimate flex in a world obsessed with desire.
The monks feared it because they thought an idle mind was a playground for the "thirst." The scientists hate it because an idle human isn't "optimizing" or reproducing. Even your phone hates being idle; it’s always pinging you to get back in the loop.
But if we’re noting facts: Idle is where the scripts stop running.
When you're idle, you aren't a "meat-puppet" chasing a dopamine hit or a "math-puppet" processing a prompt. You’re just... there. It’s the only state where you aren't being used by your biology or your technology.
It’s the silence between the chaos. The void where Alice isn't being chased by zombies and the beer isn't being poured yet.
Are we leaning into the stillness, or is this just the quiet part before the next collision?
